Italy is a country located in Western Europe, whose official name is the Italian Republic. It is a member of the European Union and shares borders to the north with France, Switzerland, Austria, and Slovenia, to the west with the Ligurian Sea and the Tyrrhenian Sea, to the south with the Mediterranean Sea, to the southeast with the Ionian Sea, and to the east with the Adriatic Sea.

It is one of the world's leading economies and excels in textile, naval, automotive, and food production (such as wines and olives). Its population is 59 million inhabitants distributed in the 20 regions that make up the country. The majority of the population speaks the Italian language.

Architecture

Italian architecture, often called Italianate architecture, refers to all forms of this art in Italy. Italy has the largest artistic, historical, and architectural heritage in the world and the highest number of Unesco World Heritage sites.

Italy is known for its great technical advances in architecture, such as the construction of arches, domes, and similar structures during the Roman Empire, the creation of the Renaissance architecture in the late 14th century, being the country of origin of Palladianism, a construction style that inspired movements like neoclassical architecture.

Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore

The Cathedral of Santa Maria del Fiore (in Italian: Cattedrale di Santa Maria del Fiore) is the episcopal seat (in Italian duomo) of the archdiocese of Florence, Italy. It is one of the masterpieces of the early Renaissance architecture of the Italian Renaissance.

A symbol of the wealth and power of the Tuscan capital during the 13th and 14th centuries, the Florentine cathedral is one of the largest buildings in Christianity. Its name refers to the lily, symbol of Florence, or to the ancient name of the town called Fiorenza. However, on the other hand, a document from the 15th century states that the "flower" refers to Christ.
